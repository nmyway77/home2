📘 Lesson 3 · 핵심 정리

의사소통확신 여부 묻고 답하기 Are you sure? / Yes, I'm (quite) sure. / No, I'm not sure. · 정보 묻기 의문사 what·when·where·which·who로 묻기

Big Idea아이디어의 힘 — 자전거·수정액(Whiteout)·웹캠 등 우리 삶을 편리하게 한 발명 이야기.

언어형식수동태 (be + p.p.) · have/has to + 동사원형 (~해야 한다).

📌 각 문항을 먼저 풀어 본 뒤 「정답 & 해설 펼치기」를 눌러 확인하세요. 정답은 펼치기 전에는 보이지 않습니다.

15칸
우리말 뜻에 맞게 빈칸에 알맞은 말을 쓰세요.
확신 여부묻고 답하기

Are you 2? 틀림없니? / Really? 정말이니?
Is that 3? 그게 사실이니?
Are you sure 4 the movie starts at seven? 영화가 7시에 시작하는 게 확실해?
Are you sure 5 [of] your answer? 네 답이 확실해?

- 7 positive. 확실해.
- Yes, I'm 8 [pretty] sure. 응, 아주 확실해.
- No, I'm 9 sure. 아니, 확실하지 않아.

A·1 정답 & 해설 펼치기
ANSWER · 정답
1 sure · 2 certain · 3 true · 4 that · 5 about · 6 sure · 7 I'm · 8 quite · 9 not · 10 like · 11 What · 12 Who · 13 Which · 14 or · 15 Where
확신: sure = certain(확실한), true(사실인), positive(확신하는). be sure that 절 / be sure about/of 명사로 쓴다. quite(꽤), not(부정).

정보 묻기: What ~ like?(어떠니?), What(무엇/어떤), Who(누가), Which ~, A or B?(A와 B 중 어느 것), Where(어디).

A·2 정답 & 해설 펼치기
ANSWER · 정답
1. What will the weather be like tomorrow? — What ~ like?로 날씨를 물음.
2. Are you sure? — 상대 말에 확신 여부를 되묻는 표현.
3. Which team will win — Which + 명사로 '어느 팀'을 물음.
4. Yes, I'm quite sure. — 1km밖에 안 되니 확신함.
A·3
괄호 안 고르기 (Role-play)
14개
괄호 안에서 어법·표현상 알맞은 말을 고르세요.

G: Look at this program, Liam. This Saturday is Science Day, and there (is / are) lots of (interesting / interested) activities.

B: You mean April 21?

G: Yes. All the activities (look / looks) fun, and they're all free.

B: All of them? Are you (sure / okay)?

G: Yes, I'm (not / quite) sure. I saw it on the poster. Anyway, (where / which) ones are you (interesting / interested) in?

B: I want (going / to go) to the drone-flying class the most. (How / Which) about you, Somi?

G: Well, I want to go to Dr. Lee's lecture (on / in) AI.

B: Oh, I like that, (too / either). Let's go to both (class / classes).

G: Let's (see / sees). The drone-flying class is on Saturday afternoon, and Dr. Lee's lecture is on Sunday morning.

B: Perfect. I (can / can't) wait.

A·3 정답 & 해설 펼치기
ANSWER · 정답
are / interesting / look / sure / quite / which / interested / to go / How / on / too / classes / see / can't
are — 복수주어 lots of activities.   interesting — 활동이 '흥미를 주는'.   look — all the activities(복수).   sure — 확신 여부.   quite — '꽤 확실해'.

which — 여러 활동 중 '어느 것'.   interested — 사람이 '관심 있는'.   to go — want + to부정사.   How about you?(너는 어때?)   on — lecture on AI(~에 대한 강의).

too — 긍정문의 '~도'.   classes — both 뒤 복수.   see — Let's + 동사원형.   can't — I can't wait(너무 기대된다).

본문 ❵ Bicycle
10곳
본문에서 어법상 어색한 부분을 모두 찾아 바르게 고쳐 쓰세요.
Bicycle
Mt. Tambora, a volcano in Indonesia, was exploded in 1815. The explosion created a hugely cloud of ash, so crops failed around the world. As a result, people didn't have enough food eat. Surprisingly, this made traveling difficultly. Why? In those days, people rode horses, but many horses killed for food. People needed a new way travel, and Karl von Drais was invented the first bicycle in Germany in 1817. Drais's bicycle had two wooden wheels but no pedals. How did them move without pedals? Well, riders simply pushed the bicycle forward with their foots. The present-day bicycle is the result of many inventors's work in the 19th century.
D·1 정답 & 해설 펼치기
ANSWER · 정답
was exploded → exploded — explode는 자동사라 수동태 불가.
hugely → huge — 명사 cloud를 꾸미는 형용사.
eat → to eat — enough food를 꾸미는 형용사적 용법 to부정사.
difficultly → difficult — make + 목적어 + 형용사 보어.
killed → were killed — 말이 죽임을 당하는 대상이므로 수동태.
way travel → way to travel — a new way를 꾸미는 to부정사.
was invented → invented — Drais가 발명한 주체이므로 능동.
them → it — 단수 Drais's bicycle를 받음.
foots → feet — foot의 불규칙 복수형.
inventors's → inventors' — -s로 끝나는 복수 소유격은 어퍼스트로피만.
D·2
본문 ❶ Whiteout
11곳
본문에서 어법상 어색한 부분을 모두 찾아 바르게 고쳐 쓰세요.
Whiteout
Whiteout was inventing by Bette Graham. In 1956, Graham was worked at a bank in Texas, USA. At that time, people had retype the whole page when they were made even a small mistake. Graham was a bad typist, so she needs a solution. One day, Graham saw some window painters. When they made a mistake, they corrected them by simply paint over it. The next day, she made her own white paint and use it correct her typing mistakes. Surprisingly, nobody didn't notice! Soon, everybody at the bank began use it.
D·2 정답 & 해설 펼치기
ANSWER · 정답
inventing → invented — 수정액은 발명된 대상이므로 수동태 was invented.
was worked → was working — 과거의 특정 시점 진행 → 과거진행형.
had retype → had to retype — '~해야 했다'는 had to + 동사원형.
were made → made — they가 실수를 하는 주체이므로 능동.
needs → needed — 앞 문장이 과거이므로 과거형.
them → it — 단수 a mistake를 받음.
paint → painting — 전치사 by 뒤에는 동명사.
use → used — made와 병렬, 과거형.
correct → to correct — '~하기 위해' 목적의 부사적 용법 to부정사.
didn't notice → noticed — nobody가 이미 부정이라 이중부정 불가.
began use → began using — begin은 동명사/to부정사를 목적어로(동사원형 불가).

The first webcam invented watch a coffee pot. In 1991, Dr. Quentin Stafford-Fraser and Dr. Paul Jardetzky was working at a computer lab in England. Working better, they needed lots of coffee. However, there were only one coffee machine in the building. So, they had to made much disappointing trips to the empty coffee pot. As a solution, the two researchers setted up a camera in front of the coffee machine. The camera took pictures of the coffee pot three time a minute. With special software, all the researcher in the building can see the pictures on their local network. No more disappointed trip!
D·3 정답 & 해설 펼치기
ANSWER · 정답
invented → was invented — 웹캠은 발명된 대상이므로 수동태.
watch → to watch — '~하기 위해' 목적의 to부정사.
was → were — 주어가 두 사람이므로 복수 be동사.
Working → To work — '더 잘 일하기 위해' 목적의 to부정사.
were → was — 단수 only one coffee machine.
made → make — had to + 동사원형.
much → many — 셀 수 있는 복수 trips.
setted → set — set은 과거형도 set(불규칙).
three time → three times — 횟수는 복수형 times.
researcher → researchers — all the + 복수명사.
can → could — 과거 서술이므로 과거형 조동사.
disappointed → disappointing — trip이 실망을 유발하는 대상.
trip → trips — no more는 가산명사를 복수로.

Bicycle
Mt. Tambora, a volcano in India, exploded in 1815. The explosion created a small cloud of ash, so crops grew around the world. As a result, animal didn't have enough food to eat. Surprisingly, this made traveling easy. Why? In those days, people rode donkeys, but many horses were saved for food. People needed a new way to travel, and Karl von Drais invented the first car in France in 1817. Drais's bicycle had three wooden wheels but no handles. How did it move with pedals? Well, riders simply pulled the bicycle backward with their feet. The present-day bicycle is the cause of many inventors' work in the 19th century.
E·1 정답 & 해설 펼치기
ANSWER · 정답
India → Indonesia (탐보라 화산은 인도네시아) · small → huge (거대한 화산재 구름) · grew → failed (농작물이 실패함) · animal → people (사람들이) · easy → difficult (여행이 어려워짐)

donkeys → horses (말을 탔음) · saved → killed (식량으로 죽임) · car → bicycle (최초의 자전거) · France → Germany (독일에서)

three → two (바퀴 두 개) · handles → pedals (페달이 없음) · with pedals → without pedals (페달 없이) · pulled → pushed (밀었음) · backward → forward (앞으로) · cause → result (결과물).

본문 ❶ Whiteout
10곳
본문의 내용과 일치하지 않는 곳을 모두 찾아 바르게 고쳐 쓰세요.
Whiteout
Whiteout was invented by Bette Graham. In 1956, Graham was working at a store in Texas, USA. At that time, people had to retype the whole page when they made even a big mistake. Graham was a good typist, so she needed a solution. One day, Graham saw some window cleaners. When they made a mistake, they corrected it by simply painting under it. The next day, she bought her own white paint and used it to correct her painting mistakes. Surprisingly, everybody noticed! Soon, nobody at the bank stopped using it.
E·2 정답 & 해설 펼치기
ANSWER · 정답
store → bank (은행에서 근무) · big → small (작은 실수에도) · good → bad (서툰 타자수) · cleaners → painters (창문 페인트공) · under → over (위에 덧칠)

bought → made (직접 만듦) · painting mistakes → typing mistakes (타자 실수) · everybody noticed → nobody noticed (아무도 못 알아챔) · nobody ... stopped → everybody ... began (모두 쓰기 시작).

Webcam

However, there was only one coffee machine in the building. So, they had to make many disappointing trips to the full coffee pot. As a solution, the two researchers set up a computer in front of the coffee machine. The camera took pictures of the coffee pot three times a day. With special hardware, all the researchers in the building could see the pictures on their public network. No more exciting trips!
E·3 정답 & 해설 펼치기
ANSWER · 정답
cup → pot (커피포트를 지켜봄) · science → computer (컴퓨터 연구실) · tea → coffee (커피가 많이 필요) · full → empty (빈 커피포트) · computer → camera (카메라를 설치)

day → minute (1분에 세 번) · hardware → software (특수 소프트웨어) · public → local (지역 네트워크) · exciting → disappointing (실망스러운 발걸음).
