📌 Lesson 1 본문 — Live Freely and Happily Ever After! (Inho's Blog)

You can work with NGOs as a teenager. It is a great way to learn about social issues and make the world better.

JAN 5 — I grew up in Jeju-do, so I had many chances to see dolphins. Then, I learned that many dolphins are in danger. Some are even endangered. I thought I should do something. So, I searched the Internet and found an NGO, Dolphin Lovers. It does various activities such as cleaning up beaches and helping dolphins. After some thought, I joined it today.

JAN 20 — Many dolphins get sick or even die because of plastic waste and fishing nets, so cleaning up beaches is important. There was a beach cleanup party today, and I took part in it. Cleaning up the beach was not easy, but I enjoyed it. Oh, I saw Aunt Sumi there. She is also a member of Dolphin Lovers. What a small world!

FEB 14 — This afternoon, I took part in a training class for Halla and Olle, two dolphins in an aquarium in Jeju-do. The latest project of Dolphin Lovers is to return them to the sea. For this, Halla and Olle first need to learn some skills. They will need them to survive in the wild. I really wanted to be part of the project. Luckily, I got the chance. I just ran some errands in the training class today, but the experience made me so excited.

APR 22 — Today, Halla and Olle went back to the sea. There was a small ceremony, and all the members of Dolphin Lovers were there. The ceremony made us quite emotional. In fact, I cried a little. Now I cannot see Halla and Olle anymore! However, I was very happy at the same time. After 10 years of hard life in an aquarium, now all the sea is their home. "Live freely and happily ever after!" I shouted and waved goodbye to them.

📌 Lesson 1 추가 독해 — What are NGOs?

An NGO (Non-Governmental Organization) is a group that works independently from the government. It usually focuses on solving social, political, or environmental problems.

NGOs try to make a positive difference in areas such as education, healthcare, human rights, and disaster relief. They often work in places where governments cannot or do not provide enough help. They fill these gaps by offering important services directly to people in need.

NGOs also have an important role in society. They raise awareness about serious issues and speak up to improve policies and laws. They work closely with local communities, other organizations, and sometimes even governments to create bigger change. For example, organizations like the Red Cross, Amnesty International, and Greenpeace are well known NGOs. The Red Cross provides humanitarian aid, Amnesty International protects human rights, and Greenpeace works to protect the environment. Most NGOs are usually funded through donations, grants, and sometimes membership fees. They depend on volunteers and dedicated professionals who care deeply about their mission. In short, NGOs play an important role in making the world a better place.

📌 Lesson 2 본문 — Text Better, Communicate Better!

Texting is the main way of communication for many people these days, especially for teenagers. Do you want to know how you can communicate better through texting? Here are some useful tips.

1. You need to know who you are talking to. Henry just sent the following text message to his mother. Henry: Mom, I want you to wait just a little longer. BRT. Do you think Henry's mother correctly understood BRT? Probably not. You can use expressions like BRT (be right there), JK (just kidding), or LOL (laughing out loud) when you text your friends. However, be careful with such expressions when you text your teachers or parents. You need to understand that some of them may not know the meanings of such expressions.

2. You need to understand that people use emojis differently. Yesterday, Jisu gave a birthday gift to her little brother Jihoo. Later, she got the following text message. Jihoo: I loved your gift. Thanks a lot. ☺ "Only one smiling face (☺)? I guess he didn't like my gift," Jisu thought. When we talk with someone, we use not only words but also facial expressions and body language. In texting, we use emojis instead. Sometimes, a little emoji like ☺ can speak louder than many words. The problem is that not everybody uses emojis in the same way. Some people like to use them a lot, and some don't. In addition, the same emojis can mean different things to different people.

3. Sometimes, you need to pick up the phone. Amy just got the following text message from her best friend Nari. Nari: Did you really talk behind my back? I want you to apologize to me. Amy wanted to text Nari and explain the situation, but she thought again. "Well, this is too much for a text," she said to herself and called Nari. Texting is okay for casual situations, but sometimes, you need to pick up the phone. This is because misunderstandings can easily happen when you text. Do you need to talk about sensitive issues with your friends? Then, don't text them. Call them instead.

※ 줄임말은 친구 간 문자에는 OK이지만, 선생님/부모님께는 의미를 모르실 수 있으므로 주의해야 함 (본문 11-12번).
46본문L2중간 변형사례 추론 — Jisu & Jihoo 이모지 오해4점
46. 다음 글에서 지수(Jisu)가 한 생각의 이유로 가장 적절한 것은?
Yesterday, Jisu gave a birthday gift to her little brother Jihoo. Later, she got the following text message. Jihoo: I loved your gift. Thanks a lot. ☺ "Only one smiling face (☺)? I guess he didn't like my gift," Jisu thought.
① 지후가 선물을 받지 못했기 때문에 ② 지후가 답장을 늦게 보냈기 때문에 ③ 지수와 지후가 이모지를 다르게 사용하기 때문에 ④ 지후가 줄임말 BRT, JK, LOL을 사용하지 않았기 때문에 ⑤ 지수가 영어 표현을 잘 모르기 때문에
정답 & 해설
정답 ③ — 같은 이모지(☺)가 사람마다 다른 의미로 받아들여지는 사례
본문 13~23번의 핵심 메시지:
"You need to understand that people use emojis differently." (13번)
"The problem is that not everybody uses emojis in the same way." (21번)

지후는 ☺ 하나만으로도 충분히 고마움을 표현했지만, 지수는 "한 개뿐? 마음에 안 들었나 봐"라고 해석한 것 — 같은 이모지를 다르게 사용하는 사례.
47본문L2중간 변형사례 추론 — Amy & Nari 결론3점
47. 다음 글의 흐름으로 보아 Amy가 나리에게 전화를 건 가장 적절한 이유는?

① 문자가 일상적인 대화에는 더 효과적이기 때문에 ② 민감한 주제는 문자로 하면 오해가 생기기 쉽기 때문에 ③ 나리가 이모지를 너무 많이 사용했기 때문에 ④ 줄임말이 친구 간에는 부적절하기 때문에 ⑤ Amy가 영어 문법에 자신이 없었기 때문에
정답 & 해설
정답 ② — 민감한 문제는 문자보다 전화가 적절
본문 30~33번 직접 근거:
"Texting is okay for casual situations, but sometimes, you need to pick up the phone."(30)
"This is because misunderstandings can easily happen when you text."(31)

※ "this is too much for a text" = 문자로 하기에는 너무 큰일/민감한 일 → 전화 선택.
48본문L2소폭 변형상관접속사 not only A but also B3점
48. 다음 빈칸 (A), (B)에 들어갈 말로 가장 적절한 것은?
When we talk with someone, we use (A) ______ words (B) ______ facial expressions and body language. In texting, we use emojis instead.
① (A) not only   (B) but also ② (A) either   (B) or ③ (A) neither   (B) nor ④ (A) both   (B) or ⑤ (A) not   (B) but
정답 & 해설
정답 ① — not only A but also B (A뿐만 아니라 B도)
본문 18번:
"we use not only words but also facial expressions and body language."

① ✓ not only A but also B = A뿐만 아니라 B도
② either A or B = A 또는 B (둘 중 하나)
③ neither A nor B = A도 B도 아닌
④ both는 both A and B로 짝
⑤ not A but B = A가 아니라 B

맥락상 단어와 표정/몸짓 둘 다 사용한다는 의미이므로 ①.
49본문L2중간 변형접속사 that — 보어절3점
49. 다음 문장의 빈칸에 공통으로 들어갈 말로 가장 적절한 것은?
· You need to understand ______ some of them may not know the meanings of such expressions.
· The problem is ______ not everybody uses emojis in the same way.
· You need to understand ______ people use emojis differently.
① if ② what ③ that ④ which ⑤ whether
정답 & 해설
정답 ③ — 명사절을 이끄는 접속사 that
본문 12, 21, 13번 모두 동일 구조:
· understand that ~ (목적어 명사절)
· The problem is that ~ (보어 명사절)
· understand that ~ (목적어 명사절)

① if/⑤ whether: ~인지 아닌지 (불확실)
② what: 선행사 포함, 뒤에 불완전절
③ that: ~라는 것 (확정 사실)
④ which: 관계대명사

모두 완전한 문장이 뒤따르고 "~라는 사실/내용"을 의미하므로 that.
50본문L2소폭 변형비교급 louder than3점
50. 다음 우리말과 같도록 빈칸에 들어갈 말로 가장 적절한 것은?
Sometimes, a little emoji like ☺ can speak ______ many words.
(때로는 ☺ 같은 작은 이모지 하나가 많은 단어들보다 더 큰 의미를 말할 수 있다.)
① loud as ② as loud ③ louder than ④ the loudest ⑤ more louder
정답 & 해설
정답 ③ — 비교급 louder + than
본문 20번:
"a little emoji like ☺ can speak louder than many words."

① loud as: as ~ as 동등비교 (불완전)
② as loud: 단독 사용 불가
③ louder than: ~보다 더 크게 (비교급+than) ✓
④ the loudest: 최상급 (셋 이상 비교)
⑤ more louder: 비교급 중복 오류 (loud는 1음절 → -er만)

※ loud → louder → loudest. more loud, more louder 모두 비표준.
51본문L2소폭 변형5형식 want + O + to-V3점
51. 다음 두 문장의 빈칸에 공통으로 들어갈 말로 가장 적절한 것은?
· Henry: Mom, I want you ______ just a little longer. BRT.
· Nari: I want you ______ to me.
① wait / apologize ② waiting / apologizing ③ to wait / to apologize ④ waited / apologized ⑤ to waiting / to apologizing
정답 & 해설
정답 ③ — want + 목적어 + to부정사
본문 7, 27번 핵심 어법:
· "I want you to wait just a little longer." (7번)
· "I want you to apologize to me." (27번)

「want + 목적어 + to+동사원형」 = ~가 …하기를 원하다 (5형식)

※ 비교: want는 to부정사를 보어로 취하는 대표 동사. ask/tell/expect/advise/allow도 동일 구조 (Section H 참고).
52본문L2중간 변형본문 흐름도 — Tip 매칭4점
