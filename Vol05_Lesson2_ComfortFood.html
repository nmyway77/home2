Letters from Our Readers: My Comfort Food

Comfort food is food that makes you feel good when you are sad, angry, or stressed out. It can also make you think of happy moments from the past. It ___(A)___ not only the stomach but also the heart. Comfort foods differ around the world. Let's see what comfort foods our international readers enjoy.

Jessica from USA

My comfort food is chicken soup. In the USA, people eat this soup when they have a cold. When I was a small child, I caught a very bad cold. My father made me a bowl of chicken soup ⓐso that I can get well. The hot soup warmed my body, and I slowly started to feel better. It was also very tasty. Now, ___(B)___ I catch a cold, I eat chicken soup.

Maria from Brazil

In Brazil, there are many dishes ⓑthat are made with cassava, a vegetable similar to a potato. I love cassava chips the most. Once when I had a bad day at school and felt stressed out, my best friend bought me a bag of cassava chips. When I started to eat the chips, my stress suddenly ___(C)___. The crisp sound of eating chips made me feel better. Now every time I'm stressed out, I eat cassava chips. Then I feel good again!

Hiroshi from Japan
In Japan, when someone feels sick, families often prepare okayu, a warm rice porridge. I remember ⓒwhich my mother made okayu for me when I had a fever last winter. She cooked it slowly for about 15 minutes, adding a little salt. After eating it, I felt warmed from inside and my body recovered faster than before. Even now, the smell of okayu reminds me of my mother's care.
Simon from France

I have many comfort foods, but I love madeleines the most. A madeleine is a small cake ⓓthat looks like a sea shell. People in France enjoy madeleines as an afternoon snack. My grandmother always makes madeleines for me when I visit her. They taste best when they come right out of the oven. Then the kitchen is filled with a sweet smell. I especially like eating her orange madeleines with a cup of tea. ⓔWhile I see or smell madeleines, I think of my grandmother.

Let me share my grandmother's special recipe with you so that you can make orange madeleines, too. Maybe madeleines will become a comfort food for you!

Recipe. Ingredients: 1 cup of flour, 2 eggs, 2/3 cup of sugar, 1/8 teaspoon of salt, 1/4 cup of butter, some orange peel.
Step 1. Melt the butter and let it cool.
Step 2. Put the eggs, sugar, and salt in a bowl and beat.
Step 3. Add the flour to the bowl and mix.
Step 4. Add the butter and orange peel to the mixture and mix.
Step 5. Pour the mixture into the madeleine pan.
Step 6. Bake in the oven for 10 to 15 minutes.

3점
문맥상 가장 적절한 단어끼리 짝지은 것은?
A madeleine is a small cake that (A)[looks/tastes] like a sea shell. They taste best when they come (B)[right out/long after] of the oven. Then the kitchen is (C)[filled/emptied] with a sweet smell.
　(A)(B)(C)

②tasteslong afteremptied

정답 & 해설 펼치기
ANSWER
정답: ① looks / right out / filled

09
어법 틀린 개수
5점
밑줄 친 ⓐ~ⓔ 중, 어법상 틀린 것의 개수는?
ⓐ~ⓔ 복기
ⓐ My father made me a bowl of chicken soup so that I can get well.
ⓑ In Brazil, there are many dishes that are made with cassava.
ⓒ I remember which my mother made okayu for me when I had a fever.
ⓓ A madeleine is a small cake that looks like a sea shell.
ⓔ While I see or smell madeleines, I think of my grandmother.
  • ①1개
  • ②2개
  • ③3개
  • ④4개
  • ⑤5개
정답 & 해설 펼치기
ANSWER
정답: ③ 3개 (ⓐ, ⓒ, ⓔ)
  • ⓐ🚨 주절 과거 made + so that절 현재 can → 시제 일치 could. "so that I could get well".
  • ⓑ주격 관계대명사 that + 복수 are ☑️
  • ⓒ🚨 remember 뒤 완전한 절 → 접속사 that. which는 선행사 필요.
  • ⓓ주격 관계대명사 that + 단수 looks ☑️
  • ⓔ🚨 "~할 때마다" 의미 → every time / whenever. while은 "~하는 동안" 시간 지속.
🔑 3대 포인트. so that + 시제 일치 / 접속사 that vs 관계대명사 which / every time vs while.

(A)~(F)를 마들렌 조리 순서대로 배열한 것은?

(C) Bake in the oven for 10 to 15 minutes.

  • ②(B)–(D)–(F)–(A)–(E)–(C)

14단어 배열 ★
5점
우리말에 맞도록 <보기>의 단어를 배열할 때 ★에 들어갈 단어는?

< 보기 >

  • ②bowl
  • ③so
  • ④that
  • ⑤could

ANSWER
정답: ④ that
완성. My father made me a bowl of chicken soup so ★that I could get well.

  • 3made (4형식 동사)
  • 4me (간접 목적어)
  • 5-8a / bowl / of / chicken (직접 목적어구)
  • 9soup (직접 목적어 명사)
  • 10so (목적 접속사 시작)
  • ★that (so that 접속사) ← 정답
  • 12I (so that절 주어)
  • 13could (조동사 과거)
  • 14get (동사원형)
  • 15well (부사)
🔑 3대 구조. make A B (4형식) + so that + S + could + V (목적) + get well (회복하다 관용구).
