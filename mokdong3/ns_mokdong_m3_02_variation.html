  • 어법 오류 3곳 심기: ⓐ which → who / ⓒ drawing → drawn / ⓓ what → that (9번 정답 근거)

  • 신규 문단 추가: bitter food(쓴맛) 관련 문단 전체 신규 + 구체 수치 "30%" 삽입 (11번 숫자 함정 근거)

Comfort Food and What It Reveals

Do you have a favorite food to eat when you feel stressed? We usually call this type of food "comfort food." Have you ever wondered what your favorite food says about you? Interestingly, psychologists explain how our food choices (A) our inner traits.

___(가)___, many people often crave sweet foods like chocolate or ice cream. Eating sweet food usually makes us feel very happy and relaxed. Studies show that people ⓐwhich prefer sweet snacks are often friendly and helpful. They like to help others and usually have a positive attitude toward life.

___(나)___, some people love salty snacks like potato chips. People who favor salty comfort foods are usually outgoing and energetic. They enjoy meeting new friends and hate being alone for a long time.

What about spicy food, like spicy Tteokbokki? Researchers found that people who enjoy spicy food are usually thrill-seekers. They like trying new things, and they ⓑenjoy taking risks in life. The strong spicy taste lets them experience a sense of excitement and adventure.

Interestingly, some individuals are drawn to bitter foods such as dark chocolate or black coffee. Research suggests that those ⓒdrawing to bitter flavors tend to have bolder personalities and may even display more unpredictable behaviors. A recent study found that bitter-food fans scored about 30% higher on tests measuring independence and self-focus compared to sweet-food lovers.

___(다)___, our parents have us eat healthy meals like salads. People who choose healthy food as their comfort food are usually organized. They care about their bodies and make careful plans for their daily lives.

So, the next time you eat your comfort food, think about your personality. You can easily guess why you naturally crave those specific flavors. Your food choice is a clear mirror ⓓwhat reflects your true inner self. Therefore, we can say that our personalities strongly ⓔinfluence our food preferences.

정답 & 해설 펼치기
ANSWER
정답: ① For example / On the other hand / Sometimes
풀이 논리.
(가) 일반 주장 → 단맛이라는 구체 예시 = For example
(나) 단맛(친근·도움) ↔ 짠맛(외향·독립) 대조 = On the other hand
(다) "부모님이 때때로 건강식을 권한다" 일반화 = Sometimes
  • ①✓ 예시 / 대조 / 일반화 세 가지 논리 관계 모두 부합.
  • ②(가) However 역접 불가 / (다) Therefore 인과 불가.
  • ③(나) As a result는 원인-결과 관계 필요.
  • ④(나) Similarly는 유사 병렬인데 내용은 대조.
  • ⑤(가) As a result 부적절.
🔑 연결사 4 범주. 예시(For example) / 대조(However, On the other hand) / 추가(In addition, Moreover) / 인과(Therefore, As a result)

4점
윗글의 내용을 한 문장으로 요약할 때, 빈칸 (A), (B)에 들어갈 말로 가장 적절한 것은?

07
어휘 (A)(B)(C) 문맥 적절
· 문맥 어휘 선택
3점
문맥상 (A), (B), (C)의 각 네모 안에서 어법·어휘에 맞는 단어로 가장 적절한 것끼리 짝지은 것은?
...Studies show that people who prefer sweet snacks are often (A)[friendly / strict] and helpful. They like to help others and usually have a positive attitude toward life.

Research suggests that those drawn to bitter flavors tend to have bolder personalities and may even display more (B)[unpredictable / traditional] behaviors.

People who choose healthy food as their comfort food are usually organized. They care about their bodies and make (C)[careful / reckless] plans for their daily lives.
　(A)(B)(C)
①friendlyunpredictablecareful
②strictunpredictablereckless
③friendlytraditionalcareful
④stricttraditionalreckless
⑤friendlyunpredictablereckless
정답 & 해설 펼치기
ANSWER
정답: ① friendly / unpredictable / careful
풀이 논리.
(A) 뒤 "helpful(남을 돕는)" + "help others" + "positive attitude"와 논리적 결합 → friendly(친근한). strict(엄격한)는 모순.
(B) 앞 "bolder personalities(더 대담한 성격)" + 쓴맛 선호자의 독특한 특성 맥락 → unpredictable(예측 불가한). traditional(전통적)은 반대.
(C) 앞 "organized(체계적)" + "care about their bodies" 맥락 → careful(신중한). reckless(무모한)는 반대.
  • ①✓ 세 맥락 모두 논리적 일관성.
  • ②~⑤한 개 이상의 단어가 문맥과 모순.
🔑 맥락 판별 핵심. (A) helpful/positive → 긍정 · (B) bolder/unpredictable → 비전통적 · (C) organized/care → 신중한 — 주변 단어의 뉘앙스로 판별.
08
흐름상 부적절 단어
· 반의어 함정
4점
다음 글의 밑줄 친 ①~⑤ 중, 글의 흐름상 적절하지 않은 것은?
Studies show that people who prefer sweet snacks are often friendly and ① helpful. They like to help others and usually have a ② negative attitude toward life. On the other hand, some people love salty snacks like potato chips. People who favor salty comfort foods are usually ③ outgoing and ④ energetic. They enjoy meeting new friends and ⑤ hate being alone for a long time.
  • ①helpful
  • ②negative
  • ③outgoing
  • ④energetic
  • ⑤hate
정답 & 해설 펼치기
ANSWER
정답: ② negative
풀이 논리. ②가 포함된 문장: "They like to help others and usually have a negative attitude toward life." → 앞 문장에서 sweet snacks 선호자들은 friendly & helpful로 긍정 묘사됨. "남을 돕는 것을 좋아하고" 다음에 부정적 태도는 논리 모순. positive가 맞음.
  • ①helpful ☑️ 친근함의 자연스러운 연결.
  • ②🚨 반의어 함정. positive → negative로 뒤집음. 앞뒤 긍정 묘사와 모순.
  • ③outgoing ☑️ "meeting new friends" + "hate being alone"과 일치.
  • ④energetic ☑️ outgoing과 자연스러운 병렬.
  • ⑤hate ☑️ "meeting new friends"를 enjoy하는 맥락과 "alone" 기피는 정합.
⚠️ 목동중 시그니처 함정. 반의어 한 단어로 문단 전체의 긍정/부정 뉘앙스를 뒤집는 함정. 주변 단어의 뉘앙스(friendly, helpful, help others)로 판별.

어법 틀린 것 개수
· ⓐ~ⓔ 밑줄
4점
윗글의 ⓐ~ⓔ 중, 어법상 틀린 것의 개수는?
ⓐ~ⓔ 복기
ⓐ ...people which prefer sweet snacks are often friendly and helpful.
ⓑ They like trying new things, and they enjoy taking risks in life.
ⓒ ...those drawing to bitter flavors tend to have bolder personalities...
ⓓ Your food choice is a clear mirror what reflects your true inner self.
ⓔ ...our personalities strongly influence our food preferences.
  • ①1개
  • ②2개
  • ③3개
  • ④4개
  • ⑤5개
정답 & 해설 펼치기
ANSWER
정답: ③ 3개 (ⓐ, ⓒ, ⓓ)
  • ⓐ🚨 which → who. 선행사 people(사람) → who/that만 가능.
  • ⓑlike와 enjoy가 and로 병렬, 둘 다 Ving 목적어 ☑️
  • ⓒ🚨 drawing → drawn. "be drawn to(~에 끌리다)" 수동 관계절 축약. those (who are) drawn → those drawn.
  • ⓓ🚨 what → that/which. 선행사(a clear mirror)가 있으므로 what 불가.
  • ⓔ주어 복수 + 부사 + 동사 원형 정상 ☑️
🔑 3대 핵심.
1. 관계대명사 선행사별 구분 (ⓐ) — 사람 → who, 사물 → which
2. 분사 수동 축약 (ⓒ) — be drawn to → drawn to
3. what vs that/which (ⓓ) — 선행사 유무로 구분
10
영영풀이 옳지 않은 것
· 본문 주요 어휘 5개
3점
윗글의 문맥에서 사용된 단어의 영영풀이로 옳지 않은 것은?
  • ①crave: to feel a very strong desire for something
  • ②outgoing: friendly and enjoying being with other people
  • ③thrill-seeker: a person who avoids exciting or dangerous experiences
  • ④bold: showing courage and confidence, not afraid of risks
  • ⑤organized: able to plan things carefully and keep them in order
정답 & 해설 펼치기
ANSWER
정답: ③ thrill-seeker
풀이 논리. thrill-seeker는 "스릴을 추구하는 사람"으로 흥분·위험을 seek(찾다)하는 사람. 선지 ③은 avoids(피하다)로 정반대 의미를 넣은 함정.
→ 올바른 정의: "a person who seeks exciting or dangerous experiences"
  • ①crave = 강하게 갈망하다 ☑️
  • ②outgoing = 외향적, 사교적 ☑️
  • ③🚨 seek ↔ avoid 반의어 치환 함정.
  • ④bold = 대담한, 용감한 ☑️
  • ⑤organized = 체계적, 정돈된 ☑️
💡 영영풀이 함정 빈출 포인트. 본문 "thrill-seekers" / "enjoy taking risks" 두 단서로 seek의 반의어를 쓰면 즉시 함정임을 판별 가능.

  • ④🚨 변동성 함정. mood 변화가 아니라 지속적 traits가 핵심.
