의견
2점
대화를 듣고, 여자의 의견으로 가장 적절한 것을 고르시오.
Script · 대본

W: You don't look happy, Jason. What's wrong?

M: It's nothing serious, but I've been feeling a bit down because of things between me and my friend, Sarah.

W: Really? I thought you two were best friends.

M: We are. We're really close. The thing is, we sometimes have different thoughts, and it's hard for me to express how I feel.

W: I understand, but keeping your feelings to yourself is only making things worse.

M: But I just don't want to make things awkward.

W: I know, but it only creates distance between you and Sarah. So, I think it's important to express your feelings honestly.

M: Do you really think being open about my emotions is better?

W: Yes. Hiding your feelings leads to misunderstandings. Telling her how you truly feel will help solve the problem.

① 친구 사이라도 예의를 갖추어 행동해야 한다. ② 논쟁 상황에서는 잠시 침묵을 지키는 것이 좋다. ③ 자신의 감정을 솔직하게 표현하는 것이 중요하다. ④ 상대방의 입장에서 먼저 생각하고 이해해야 한다. ⑤ 의견이 다를 때는 이성적으로 판단하려 노력해야 한다.
정답 & 해설 펼치기
ANSWER · 정답
③ 자신의 감정을 솔직하게 표현하는 것이 중요하다.
여자는 일관되게 "감정을 솔직히 표현하라"는 의견을 반복:
· "keeping your feelings to yourself is only making things worse" (감정을 숨기면 더 나빠진다)
· "it's important to express your feelings honestly" (솔직히 표현하는 것이 중요하다)
· "Hiding your feelings leads to misunderstandings" (감추면 오해로 이어진다)
세 번 강조 → 핵심 의견 = ③.

요지
2점
다음을 듣고, 남자가 하는 말의 요지로 가장 적절한 것을 고르시오.
Script · 대본

M: Hello, I'm doctor Robert Morgan. Today, I'm going to talk about how to make a good choice. Let me give you an example. Imagine you are trying to choose something in a store. You might think that spending more time will lead to a better decision. But research shows that your initial choice in the store is often the best one. That's because it reflects your true preference before being influenced by outside factors. When you think too much about every possible outcome and keep changing, you struggle with even simple decisions and lose confidence. That's why trusting your instinct about your first choice can actually lead to a better result in the end.

① 선택의 기준은 개인의 필요와 처한 상황에 따라 달라진다. ② 결정 상황에서 처음 정한 선택이 가장 바람직한 경우가 많다. ③ 빠르고 정확한 판단은 분석적 사고력이 뒷받침될 때 가능하다. ④ 중요한 결정에는 주변 사람들의 조언을 구하는 것이 도움이 된다. ⑤ 하나의 결론만 고집하기보다 여러 가능성을 살피는 것이 좋다.
정답 & 해설 펼치기
ANSWER · 정답
② 결정 상황에서 처음 정한 선택이 가장 바람직한 경우가 많다.
핵심 두 문장:
· "your initial choice in the store is often the best one"
· "trusting your instinct about your first choice can actually lead to a better result in the end"
→ 처음 직감(first choice)을 따르는 것이 더 좋은 결과로 이어진다는 주장. ⑤("여러 가능성을 살피는 것이 좋다")는 본문과 정반대 — 본문은 "지나치게 많이 생각하면 자신감을 잃는다"고 함.
주요 어휘 initial 처음의 · preference 선호 · outcome 결과 · struggle with ~로 고심하다 · confidence 자신감 · instinct 본능, 직감

그림 일치
2점
대화를 듣고, 그림에서 대화의 내용과 일치하지 않는 것을 고르시오.
※ 호텔 객실 — 침대 3개·코너 램프·바다 풍경(③ 그림)·둥근 테이블·전기 찻주전자
Script · 대본

M: Judy, take a look at this picture of the hotel room I booked. What do you think?

W: It looks great! Is it for our family trip?

M: Yes. Since there will be three of us, I decided on this room.

W: Then a room with three beds seems perfect.

M: Exactly. And the lamp in the corner will be useful at night.

W: Right. Also, the window seems to offer an ocean view.

M: That was one of the main reasons I chose this room.

W: I see. And I think the round table can be a nice place to enjoy some snacks.

M: I agree. There's even an electric teapot on the table, so we can enjoy some tea, too.

① 침대 3개 ✓ ② 코너 램프 ✓ ③ 창문 너머 바다 풍경 ✗ ④ 둥근 테이블 ✓ ⑤ 그림(액자) → 창문이어야 ✗
정답 & 해설 펼치기
ANSWER · 정답
⑤ — 대화에서는 "창문이 바다 풍경을 제공한다(the window seems to offer an ocean view)"고 했으므로, 그림은 창문 너머에 바다 풍경이 보여야 함. 그러나 그림에는 벽에 걸린 액자(painting)로 그려져 있어 불일치.
대본 대조:
① "three of us … a room with three beds" ✓
② "the lamp in the corner" ✓
③ "the window seems to offer an ocean view" — 창문 자체가 바다 풍경 ✓
④ "the round table … nice place to enjoy some snacks" ✓
⑤ "an electric teapot on the table" ✓

※ 일반적으로 이 유형은 대본에 명시된 5개 항목 중 그림에 잘못 그려진 것을 찾는 형식. 공식 정답은 ⑤로, 그림에 표시된 ⑤ 위치(전기 찻주전자)가 대본의 묘사와 다른 모양으로 표현된 부분.

Script · 대본

정답 & 해설 펼치기
ANSWER · 정답
① 회사에서 접속이 불가능해서
남자가 가능한 이유 4가지를 차례로 제시 → 모두 부인:

· 스마트폰 앱 → 오히려 "really liked"

최종 이유: "my company recently changed its data security policy, so I can't access your cloud service at work" — 회사 보안 정책 변경으로 접속 불가.

From Data to Insight · 본문 The emergence of the social sciences in the nineteenth century and the ability to work with large datasets created demand for new ways of visualizing information. Processed numerical information was best expressed in tables, charts, and graphs. Mathematics, natural sciences, and social sciences that employed statistics were at the (a) forefront of the development of charts and graphs. History was a consumer, not a designer, of most of these new visualizations — and mostly a sparing consumer at that, since economic and social history lagged behind political history as an area of research. Simple charts and graphs were not difficult to interpret, and their visual conventions became part of what any (b) ordinary reader would be expected to follow. As statistical analysis became more (c) sophisticated, the visualizations that resulted became more and more central to the argument. In some cases, the visualization made interpretation possible. These success stories (d) demonstrated the worth of statistical analysis and visualization. Perhaps the most notable example is John Snow's map of the incidence of cholera in an 1854 London outbreak, which helped plot the source of the outbreak at a single water pump in the neighborhood. Snow's cholera map showed that visualizations could serve as both narrative and analysis. Authors began to experiment with ways of using visual clues to tell complex stories about events, increasing the amount of information that could be conveyed in a small space and thereby (e) intensifying the limitations of two dimensions in print.
