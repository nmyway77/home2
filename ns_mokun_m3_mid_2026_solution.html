  • ⓐbreak down (고장 나다) — 오래된 기계가 곧 고장 날 것이고 비싼 대체품이 필요. ✓

• I happen to know the answer. (나는 우연히 답을 안다.)
• Do you happen to have a pen? (혹시 펜 있어?)

[6~7] 다음을 읽고 물음에 답하시오
Globalization and Local Produce

Globalization has enriched our diets with diverse foods from all around the world. However, the system behind global food production causes huge damage to our planet. It accounts for 26% of global greenhouse gas emissions, 70% of freshwater use, and half of the world's habitable land. In this situation, it is easy to feel overwhelmed about how to create a meaningful change. Fortunately, _____(A)_____.

One of the main ways local produce helps the environment is reducing "food miles." Food miles refer to the distance that food travels from its production center to its place of sale. Traditional food transportation involves trucks, ships, and planes, which significantly contribute to rising greenhouse gas emissions. In fact, on average, produce in the United States *imported from other countries travels about 1,500 miles before it reaches the dinner table. By choosing local produce, you can significantly lower these transportation emissions. This is because local produce generally has fewer "food miles" than produce transported from other districts or imported from abroad.

Beyond the environmental benefits, purchasing local produce also helps build a more sustainable community. Studies show that money spent on local produce tends to stay within the community, which helps the local economy stay strong. Local food systems directly support local farmers and businesses by creating jobs and boosting employment rates.

* imported: 수입된

Body Image

Have you heard the term "body image"? It means "the way you see your own body." A lot of teens today have a negative body image. They often think they're too fat or too thin compared to others. This issue is especially serious in South Korea. According to the Korea Youth Risk Behavior Survey by the government, there is a shocking finding: nearly half of Korean teenage girls who are actually at a perfectly normal and healthy weight think themselves as "*overweight." This distorted perception is largely influenced by social media and the strict beauty standards shown on TV. When students constantly compare themselves to heavily edited photos of celebrities or influencers, they are likely to go on extreme and unnecessary diets, which damage both their physical and mental health.

To solve this problem, we need practical steps. First, always remind yourself that most online images are filtered and do not reflect reality. Most importantly, the biggest change starts within your mind. Accept your body as it is and give yourself praise every day. Remember, there is only one you, so don't compare yourself to others. Loving yourself can make a big difference in your life.

* overweight: 과체중의

The Decoy Effect

Companies often use psychological tricks to increase their profits. A famous pricing strategy refers to the "*decoy effect." Imagine you are buying popcorn at a movie theater: a small size is $3, and a large size is $7. The large one might seem too expensive. However, if the theater sells a medium size for $6.50, it will make you buy the large one, which looks like a great deal. Instead of questioning whether $7 is too much for popcorn, people focus only on the tiny 50-cent difference between the medium and large. As a result, they ___(A)___ spend more money than they planned, believing they made a right choice. In this case, the medium size is just a "decoy." Marketers associate this strategy with human **irrationality. Because humans often ___(B)___ comparing options right in front of them rather than judging the true value, this clever pricing trick accounts for a massive increase in a company's sales.

* decoy: 미끼　** irrationality: 비합리성
