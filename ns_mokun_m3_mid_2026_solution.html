정답 & 해설 펼치기
ANSWER
정답: ④ keeps
완성 문장. It tells me to drink water every two hours, which ★keeps me hydrated all the time.

배열 순서.
  • ①to (부정사 — 'tell + 목적어 + to부정사' 구조)
  • ②drink (부정사 동사)
  • ③two (이미 제공)
  • ④hours (이미 제공)
  • ⑤which (앞 문장 전체를 선행사로 받는 계속적 용법)
  • ★keeps (주어 which의 동사, 'keep + O + 형용사' 구조)
  • ⑥me hydrated (목적어 + 상태 보어)
핵심 문법. 관계대명사 which의 계속적 용법은 앞 문장 전체를 선행사로 받을 수 있으며, 이때 that은 쓸 수 없다. 또한 keep + 목적어 + 형용사 구조("~을 ...한 상태로 유지하다")를 정확히 알아야 keeps 뒤의 어순을 맞출 수 있다.
💡 학습 포인트. 목운중은 "서답형 영작"을 선택형으로 전환한 이 유형을 시그니처로 활용함. 관계대명사 + keep/make/find 5형식 조합을 평소에 훈련해 두면 반복 대응 가능.

대화 내용 일치
· 교과서 L&T 2 변형
3점
윗글의 내용과 일치하는 것은?
[A]
Ben: Hey, Amy! What are you holding?
Amy: Hi, Ben. It's a skateboard. I'm going skateboarding.
Ben: Wow! I didn't know that you could skateboard. How did you learn to skateboard?
Amy: I was looking for a new hobby and joined a local skateboarding club last week.
Ben: I see. ___
Amy: It's really fun! It helped me make new friends, too.
Ben: How?
Amy: I go skateboarding with other members of the club, and we share tips with one another.
[B]
Lia: Did you make this shelf yourself? It's amazing!
Jack: Thanks. I started making furniture last year.
Lia: Cool! ___
Jack: It was hard at first, but now I love it. I feel so confident after I finish making something.
Lia: That's great. I think you're really good at it.
Jack: Thanks. I guess I found a new talent. I think it's good to try new things.
Lia: Exactly. Having new experiences lets us find new talents.
  • ①In [A], Ben knew that Amy could skateboard and wanted to join a skateboarding club together.
  • ②In [A], Amy started participating in a local skateboarding club last week while she was looking for a new job.
  • ③In [A], Amy is not satisfied with joining a skateboarding club because she couldn't make new friends.
  • ④In [B], Lia feels so confident when she starts making something such as a shelf.
  • ⑤In [B], Lia shows agreement with Jack's opinion on trying new things by saying "Exactly."
정답 & 해설 펼치기
ANSWER
정답: ⑤
풀이 논리. 대화 끝부분 Jack의 "I think it's good to try new things"에 대해 Lia가 "Exactly"로 동의하며 동일 주장을 확장("Having new experiences lets us find new talents"). 명백한 agreement(동의) 표현.
  • ①Ben은 Amy가 skateboard 할 수 있는 줄 몰랐음 ("I didn't know that you could skateboard"). "같이 가입하고 싶다"는 언급 없음.
  • ②🚨 함정 — 단어 바꿔치기. Amy는 "new hobby(취미)"를 찾고 있었는데 선지는 "new job(직업)"으로 바꿔 치환. 한 단어 차이로 오답 함정.
  • ③Amy는 "It's really fun! It helped me make new friends"라며 만족하고 친구도 만듦. 정반대.
  • ④🚨 함정 — 인물 + 시점. Confident 한 사람은 Lia가 아니라 Jack. 그리고 "after I finish making something"이지 "starts"가 아님.
  • ⑤정답. Lia가 "Exactly"로 Jack의 "try new things is good" 견해에 동의.
⚠️ 변형 포인트. 원본 교과서 대화에는 "I joined a local skateboarding club last month"였는데, 시험지는 "I was looking for a new hobby and joined... last week"로 확장. 추가된 "looking for a new hobby"가 선지 ②의 함정(→ job) 재료로 쓰임. 원본만 외운 학생은 ②의 "looking for"만 보고 속기 쉬움.

[B] Lia: Cool! ______
Jack: It was hard at first, but now I love it. I feel so confident after I finish making something.
  • ①How are you finding it?
  • ②What do you think of it?
  • ③What would you like to do?
  • ④What are you looking forward to?
  • ⑤How long have you been doing it?
정답 & 해설 펼치기
ANSWER
정답: ① How are you finding it?  +  ② What do you think of it?
풀이 논리. 빈칸 뒤 응답이 [A] "It's really fun!" / [B] "It was hard at first, but now I love it"로 그 활동에 대한 느낌·의견을 말함. 따라서 빈칸은 "그것을 어떻게 느끼니? / 어떻게 생각하니?" 같은 의견·소감을 묻는 질문이어야 함. 교과서 원본의 "How do you like it?"과 동의 표현.
  • ①How are you finding it? = "어떻게 느끼고 있니?" (진행형 형태지만 How do you like it?와 동의).
  • ②What do you think of it? = "그것에 대해 어떻게 생각하니?" (의견 질문의 대표 표현).
  • ③What would you like to do? = "무엇을 하고 싶니?" — 희망 활동을 묻는 질문. 문맥 불일치.
  • ④What are you looking forward to? = "무엇을 기대하니?" — 미래 기대. 문맥 불일치.
  • ⑤How long have you been doing it? = "얼마나 오래 했니?" — 기간을 묻는 질문. 뒤에 기간 응답이 와야 하는데 감상이 옴.
💬 동의 표현 정리.
"How do you like it?" ≈ "How are you finding it?" ≈ "What do you think of it?" ≈ "How's it going?"
모두 어떤 것에 대한 느낌·의견을 묻는 표현.

*replacement: 대체품
  • ①call off
  • ②happen to
  • ③carry on
  • ④break down
  • ⑤compensate for
정답 & 해설 펼치기
ANSWER
정답: ② happen to
풀이 논리. "happen to ~"는 "우연히 ~하다"라는 뜻으로 뒤에 동사원형이 와야 한다. 보기 ⓐ~ⓔ의 빈칸은 모두 명사 목적어 앞에 오거나 단독으로 쓰여야 하므로 happen to가 들어갈 자리가 없다.
  • ⓐbreak down (고장 나다) — 오래된 기계가 곧 "고장 날 것"이고 비싼 대체품 필요 ☑️
  • ⓑcompensate for (보완하다, 상쇄하다) — 경험 부족을 "보완하기 위해" 열심히 연습함 ☑️
  • ⓒcarry on (계속하다) — 시즌 끝까지 "계속하기로" 결심 ☑️
  • ⓓcall off (취소하다) — 폭풍으로 모든 항공편 "취소" ☑️
  • ⓔcarry on과 같은 맥락의 다른 숙어가 들어갈 자리 — 실제로는 compensate for도 적용 가능한 자리("불안감을 상쇄하기 위해")로 출제자가 중복 허용. 어느 쪽이든 happen to는 불가.
🔑 happen to의 쓰임.
• I happen to know the answer. (나는 우연히 답을 안다.)
• Do you happen to have a pen? (혹시 펜 가지고 있니?)
→ 항상 happen to + 동사원형. 이 문제의 빈칸들은 모두 목적어(명사) 앞에 오는 타동사구 자리라 happen to 불가.
💡 75숙어 완벽 체크. 목운중은 프린트 75숙어를 직접 3문항(5·9·23번)에서 활용. 숙어의 의미 + 문법 구조(뒤에 명사/동사원형/동명사)까지 알아야 이런 함정 대응 가능.

· 외부지문 SR3 Local Produce
3점
윗글의 흐름상 빈칸 (A)에 들어갈 말로 가장 적절한 것은?
Globalization has enriched our diets with diverse foods from all around the world. However, the system behind global food production causes huge damage to our planet. It accounts for 26% of global greenhouse gas emissions, 70% of freshwater use, and half of the world's habitable land. In this situation, it is easy to feel overwhelmed about how to create a meaningful change. Fortunately, _____(A)_____.

One of the main ways local produce helps the environment is reducing "food miles." Food miles refer to the distance that food travels from its production center to its place of sale. Traditional food transportation involves trucks, ships, and planes, which significantly contribute to rising greenhouse gas emissions. In fact, on average, produce in the United States *imported from other countries travels about 1,500 miles before it reaches the dinner table. By choosing local produce, you can significantly lower these transportation emissions. This is because local produce generally has fewer "food miles" than produce transported from other districts or imported from abroad.

Beyond the environmental benefits, purchasing local produce also helps build a more sustainable community. Studies show that money spent on local produce tends to stay within the community, which helps the local economy stay strong. Local food systems directly support local farmers and businesses by creating jobs and boosting employment rates.
*imported: 수입된
  • ①the key to saving the Earth is closer to home than you think
  • ②eating more vegetables and less meat helps protect the Earth
  • ③the advances of AI in agriculture can make the world greener
  • ④you can enjoy diverse global foods at much cheaper prices
  • ⑤global food companies agreed to develop sustainable ways of growing produce
정답 & 해설 펼치기
ANSWER
정답: ① the key to saving the Earth is closer to home than you think
풀이 논리. 빈칸 바로 다음 문단에서 "local produce(지역 농산물)"가 환경 보호의 해답이라고 설명. local = closer to home(집 가까이에) 이라는 paraphrase. 원본 결론 문장 "shopping for local produce starts right in your local town"의 의미를 정확히 재해석한 표현.
  • ①정답. "지구를 지키는 해답은 생각보다 가까이에 있다" — 뒤에 이어질 local produce 설명과 완벽히 연결.
  • ②채식(vegetables/meat) 언급 없음. 주제가 "로컬 vs 수입"이지 "식물 vs 육류"가 아님.
  • ③AI 농업 언급 전혀 없음.
  • ④🚨 함정. 글 전체가 "수입품의 단점"이므로 "저렴한 수입 식품을 즐길 수 있다"는 정반대.
  • ⑤글로벌 식품 회사 협약 언급 없음.
🔑 paraphrase 포인트. "local" ↔ "closer to home" / "key" = 해결책(solution). 본문에서 "shopping for local produce"가 직접 언급되므로, 선지 중 지역성(locality)을 함축하는 표현이 정답.

5점
<보기>의 ⓐ~ⓕ 중, 윗글의 내용과 일치하는 것의 개수는?
< 보기 >
ⓐ Thanks to globalization, we now have enriched diets with imported foods from foreign countries, which is good for protecting the environment.
ⓑ Global food production, which takes up less than a quarter of global greenhouse gas emissions, leads to huge damage to the Earth.
ⓒ Choosing local produce can lower transportation emissions because it travels a shorter distance from the production center to the point of sale than produce imported from abroad.
ⓓ Traditional food transportation such as trucks, ships, and planes hardly increases greenhouse gas emissions.
ⓔ Money spent on local produce is not likely to leak outside the community, which supports the local economy.
ⓕ Purchasing local produce can benefit the local economy by depriving locals of job opportunities.
  • ①2개
  • ②3개
  • ③4개
  • ④5개
  • ⑤6개
정답 & 해설 펼치기
ANSWER
정답: ① 2개 (ⓒ, ⓔ)
풀이 논리. 6개 보기 중 본문과 일치하는 것은 ⓒ(지역 농산물의 food miles 짧음) + ⓔ(지역 경제 지원) 2개뿐. 나머지 4개는 모두 본문 내용과 반대이거나 왜곡됨.
  • ⓐ반대. 본문은 세계화로 인한 수입 식품이 환경에 "huge damage"라고 명시. "environment에 good"은 정반대.
  • ⓑ숫자 함정. 본문은 "26%"인데 선지는 "less than a quarter(25% 미만)"로 잘못 표현. 26%는 1/4보다 큼.
  • ⓒ일치. 본문의 "local produce generally has fewer food miles than produce transported from other districts or imported from abroad"와 정확히 일치.
  • ⓓ반의어 함정. 본문은 "significantly contribute to rising greenhouse gas emissions"인데 선지는 "hardly increases"로 완전히 반대. significantly ↔ hardly.
  • ⓔ일치. 본문 "money spent on local produce tends to stay within the community, which helps the local economy stay strong"의 paraphrase.
  • ⓕ반대. 본문은 "creating jobs and boosting employment rates"(일자리 창출)인데 선지는 "depriving locals of job opportunities"(일자리 박탈)로 정반대. creating ↔ depriving.

• ⓐ: 다른 용례로 속임 (chamber는 다의어 - 공식 방 vs 해부학적 구획)
• ⓒ: 반의어로 속임 (similar ↔ different)
• ⓕ: 반의어로 속임 (crosswise ↔ lengthwise)
💡 영영풀이 암기 전략. 목운중은 프린트 영영풀이를 그대로 정답으로 쓰거나 살짝 비틀어 오답으로 씀. 단어의 정확한 의미뿐 아니라 반의어 페어(resemble↔differ, crosswise↔lengthwise)까지 알아야 함정 회피 가능.

윗글의 내용과 일치하는 것을 두 개 고르면?
A walnut looks surprisingly similar to the human brain. Like the brain, a walnut has two halves, called *hemispheres, and its surface has many folds and wrinkles. These wrinkles in the brain are not just a random pattern — they actually increase the brain's surface area, allowing it to pack in more neurons.

In fact, the human brain contains about 86 billion neurons, and these neurons need to be arranged efficiently to process information quickly. The folded structure of the brain expands the surface area dramatically. If the brain were smooth, it would require a much larger skull to hold the same number of neurons.

Walnuts are rich in omega-3 fatty acids, which are essential for brain health. Scientific research suggests that eating walnuts regularly may support cognitive function and reduce the risk of brain-related diseases. They also contain antioxidants that help protect brain cells from oxidative damage.

Interestingly, walnuts have been linked to improved memory and learning in several studies. The nutrients found in walnuts help maintain healthy communication between brain cells, which is key to learning new information and storing memories.
*hemisphere: 반구
  • ①A walnut and the human brain both have three halves, each containing many folds.
  • ②The wrinkles on the brain are a random pattern that has no functional purpose.
  • ③The folds in the human brain expand its surface area to hold about 86 billion neurons.
  • ④The folded structure of the brain allows many neurons to fit without needing a much larger skull.
  • ⑤Walnuts mainly contain vitamin C, which improves memory and learning.

  • ①🚨 숫자 함정. "three halves"는 논리 오류. 본문은 "two halves, called hemispheres"(두 반구). halves는 복수형일 뿐 개수는 2개.

  • ③일치. "folds ... increase the brain's surface area, allowing it to pack in more neurons" + "about 86 billion neurons"의 결합 ☑️
  • ④일치. "If the brain were smooth, it would require a much larger skull" → 반대로 말하면 "접힌 구조 덕분에 더 큰 두개골이 필요 없음" ☑️
