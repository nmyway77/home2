1. 어법 GRAMMAR

1-1. 간접의문문 — 의문사가 있는 경우

핵심 공식: 의문사 + 주어 + 동사 (평서문 어순)
★ 직접의문문(의문사 + 동사 + 주어) → 간접의문문(의문사 + 주어 + 동사)

Q. Why were you late this morning?

→ Can you tell me why you were late this morning?

▶ 오늘 아침 왜 늦었는지 말해줄 수 있어?

Q. Where does he usually have his meals?

→ I wonder where he usually has his meals.

▶ 그가 보통 어디서 식사를 하는지 궁금하다.

★ does → has (3인칭 단수 평서문 어순)

⚠️ 특수 규칙: think / believe / suppose / guess / imagine 류 동사
추측·생각 동사 + 간접의문문 → 의문사를 문장 맨 앞으로 이동!

Do you think + How much did this laptop cost?

→ How much do you think this laptop cost?

▶ 이 노트북이 얼마라고 생각해?

Do you imagine + What will be different in the future?

→ What do you imagine will be different in the future?

▶ 미래에 무엇이 달라질 것이라고 생각해?

💡 의문사가 주어인 경우 → 어순 변화 없음!
예) What happened? → I don't know what happened.

1-2. 간접의문문 — 의문사가 없는 경우 (if / whether)

핵심 공식: if / whether + 주어 + 동사 (~인지 아닌지)

Q. Does he like spicy food?

→ I don't know whether/if he likes spicy food.

▶ 그가 매운 음식을 좋아하는지 모른다.

★ does → likes (3인칭 단수 평서문 어순)

⚠️ whether만 가능한 경우 (if 사용 불가)
  1. to부정사 앞: She is considering whether to keep her job. (if 불가)
  2. or not 바로 뒤: I'm not sure whether or not he will come.
  3. 전치사 뒤: It depends on whether she agrees.
⚠️ if/whether 절에서 will 사용 규칙
• 부사절(조건절 등)에서는 will 못 씀 — 현재시제로 미래 표현
• 명사절(주어/목적어/보어)에서는 미래 표현 시 will 사용

📘 직접·간접의문문 변환 연습

인물직접 의문문✅ 간접의문문 정답
예시Am I going in the opposite direction?if/whether he's going in the opposite direction
SomiWhat should I have for lunch?what she should have for lunch
MikeHow can I get to the museum?how he can get to the museum
MinhoWill she like my gift?if/whether she will like his gift
SueWhen will the store open?when the store will open

✅ 완성 문장 (명사절 → will 사용)

  1. Somi is wondering what she should have for lunch.
  2. Mike wants to find out how he can get to the museum.
  3. Minho isn't certain if/whether she will like his gift.
  4. Sue is thinking about when the store will open.

1-3. to부정사 + 명사 (전치사 필요·불필요)

핵심 공식
• 사물(A) + to부정사 → 전치사 불필요 (명사가 직접 목적어)
• 사람(B) + to부정사 → 전치사 필요 (with, on, in 등)
수식받는 명사구조전치사
사람 (someone, friend)to share + 사물 + with✅ with 필요
사물 (something, food)to share❌ 불필요

📘 비교 예문

I need someone to share my lunch with. (with 필요)

I have something to share. (with 불필요)

He has no one to share his life with. (사람 → with 필요)

She brought food to share. (사물 → with 불필요)

💡 -thing/-body/-one 어순: 대명사 + 형용사 + to부정사
예) nothing interesting to do / anything special to wear

2They need a bigger houseto live in.그들은 살 더 큰 집이 필요하다.

📌 핵심 정리

  • 전치사 불필요: to meet / to share / to upload / to eat / to run (명사가 to부정사의 직접 목적어)
  • 전치사 필요: to sit on / to live in / to talk with / to write on (명사가 전치사의 목적어)
  • -thing 어순: nothing interesting to do / anything special to wear (대명사 + 형용사 + to부정사)

1-4. 사역동사 (make / have / let) + 준사역 (get / help)

사역동사 = '~가 ~하게 시키다'
목적격 보어에 동사원형을 쓰는 사역동사: make · have · let (3개)
동사어감의미
make가장 강한 어감~하게 시키다 (강제)
have가벼운 지시~하게 시키다, ~하게 하다
let허가~하도록 허락하다

📘 사역동사 예문

  1. Dad made me go to bed early. (아빠는 내가 일찍 자게 했다)
  2. She had her kid clean his room. (그녀는 자녀가 방을 청소하게 했다)
  3. The teacher will let us play soccer. (선생님은 축구를 하도록 허락할 것이다)
⚠️ 준사역동사 (get / help)
• get + 목적어 + to부정사 (to 필수)
• help + 목적어 + (to) 동사원형 (to 생략 가능)

I'll get Andrew to call you. (Andrew가 너에게 전화하도록 할게)

Can you help me (to) find my glasses? (안경 찾는 것 좀 도와줄래요?)

✅ 어법 오류 고치기

  1. My father had me fixed the bike. → had me fix the bike.
  2. Jason let me to use his tablet PC. → let me use his tablet PC.
  3. The teacher made their write the report. → made them write the report.
  4. How can I get my students listen to me? → get my students to listen to me?

3. 대화체 DIALOGUES

3-1. Have you ever ~? (경험 묻기)

경험 묻기: Have you (ever) + p.p ~? (~해본 적 있어?)
답하기: Yes, I have. / No, I haven't.

📘 대화 1 — Spanish food

G: Have you ever had Spanish food before?

↳ 스페인 음식을 전에 먹어본 적 있어?

B: No, I haven't. Have you tried it?

↳ 아니, 없어. 너는 먹어봤어?

G: Yes, I have. I hope you can try it sometime. It's really good.

↳ 응, 있어. 언젠가 한번 먹어봐. 정말 맛있어.

B: I will. For now, I'll just buy this Spanish recipe book.

↳ 그럴게. 지금은 일단 이 스페인 요리책을 살게.

📘 대화 2 — Another country

B: Have you ever visited another country?

↳ 다른 나라를 방문해 본 적 있어?

G: No, I haven't. Have you?

B: Yes, I've been to France. I hope you can travel to another country sometime.

↳ 응, 프랑스에 가본 적 있어. 언젠가 다른 나라를 여행할 수 있길 바라.

G: Yes, I really want to visit Canada. Look! This book about Canada looks very interesting.

📘 대화 3 — Book / Movie about the moon

G: You should read this book about the moon. It's really interesting.

B: I know. I've already read it.

G: You did? How about the movie? Have you also seen the movie about the book?

B: No, I haven't.

G: Well, it's even better than the book. I hope you can see the movie soon.

📘 대화 4 — Piano

G: I'm going to buy this CD. I love listening to piano music. (= to listen)

B: Me, too. I also enjoy playing the piano.

G: Really? So you can play the piano?

B: Yes. How about you?

G: Well, I've never learned how to play.

B: It's fun. I hope you'll have a chance to learn.

3-2. I hope (that) 주어 + 동사 구문

I hope (that) 주어 + 동사 → 너가 ~하길 바란다.
★ 5형식 to부정사 구문(hope + 목적어 + to부정사) 못 씀!
⚠️ 주의:
I hope you to visit there. ❌
I hope (that) you can visit there. ✅

📘 예문 모음

  • I hope you can try it sometime. (언젠가 한번 먹어봐)
  • I hope you can travel to another country. (다른 나라를 여행할 수 있길 바라)
  • I hope you can see the movie soon. (빨리 영화를 볼 수 있길 바라)
  • I hope you'll have a chance to learn. (배울 기회가 생기길 바라)
  • I hope you become a good animal doctor someday. (훌륭한 동물 의사가 되길 바라)
  • I hope my story can inspire you. (나의 이야기가 영감을 주길 바라)

3-3. 인터뷰 대화 — 동물 의사 꿈

B: I really liked your book about training dogs.

↳ 저는 당신의 개 훈련에 관한 책을 정말 좋아했어요.

W: Thank you. Do you like dogs?

B: Yes, I do. I love all kinds of animals.

W: Have you ever thought of becoming an animal doctor?

↳ 동물 의사가 되는 것을 생각해본 적 있나요?

B: Yes, I have. I really want to become an animal doctor.

W: What are you doing to achieve your goal?

↳ 목표를 이루기 위해 무엇을 하고 있나요?

B: I'm doing volunteer work at the local animal house.

W: That's good. What else are you doing?

B: I'm also watching a lot of TV shows about animals.

W: You're doing great! I hope you become a good animal doctor someday.

B: Thank you.

3-4. 밸런스 게임 (Would you rather)

What would you rather have, A or B? = A 와 B 중 어느 것을 갖고 싶어?
답: I'd rather have A. (= 나는 A를 갖고 싶어)
맞장구: Me, too! / Same here! (= 나도 그래!)

3-5. 외식·주문 (Eating Out & Ordering)

▶ Before you leave your home (외출 전 준비)

표현의미·정의예문

표현의미·정의예문

4. 독해 READING

4-1. David Parker 이야기 — 사진작가의 꿈

1. Hello, everyone. My name is David Parker, and I'm a photographer.
여러분, 안녕하세요. 나의 이름은 David Parker이고, 나는 사진작가입니다.

2. Today, I'm going to tell you how I found my dream and realized it.
오늘 나는 어떻게 내가 꿈을 찾아서 실현했는지 이야기하려고 해요.

3. I hope my story can inspire you.
나의 이야기가 여러분에게 영감을 주길 바라요.

4. When I was young, I loved stars.
나는 어렸을 때, 별을 사랑했어요.

5. I also liked taking pictures.
나는 또한 사진 찍는 것을 좋아했어요.

6. However, I never thought these things could lead to a job.
하지만 나는 이것들이 직업으로 이어질 수 있다고 전혀 생각하지 못했어요.

7. In fact, I didn't have a dream at all.
사실은 나는 아예 꿈이 없었어요.

8. When I had to decide on a major in college, I chose engineering.
대학에서 전공을 정해야 했을 때, 공학을 선택했어요.

9. Being an engineer looked OK.
엔지니어가 되는 것이 괜찮아 보였어요.

10. After college, I got a job at an engineering company.
대학 졸업 후 엔지니어링 회사에 취직했어요.

11. It was a stable job, but I didn't know whether I really enjoyed it.
안정적인 직업이었지만, 정말 좋아하는지 알 수 없었어요.

12. Everything changed when I went on vacation to Iceland one winter.
아이슬란드로 휴가를 갔던 어느 겨울에 모든 것이 바뀌었어요.

13. There I got a chance to see the Northern Lights.
그곳에서 북극광을 볼 수 있는 기회가 있었죠.

14. The lights were amazing, and I took many pictures of the dancing lights in the sky.
빛들은 경이로웠고, 하늘에서 춤추는 빛들의 사진을 많이 찍었어요.

15. For the first time in many years, I could feel my heart beating fast.
수년 만에 처음으로, 내 심장이 빠르게 뛰는 것을 느낄 수 있었어요.

16. After I came back, I entered a photo contest with the pictures I took in Iceland.
돌아와서 아이슬란드에서 찍은 사진으로 사진 경연 대회에 참가했어요.

17. Surprisingly, I won first prize, and this gave me a chance to think about my life.
놀랍게도 1등상을 받았고, 이 일은 인생을 생각해볼 기회를 주었어요.

18. I realized that taking pictures made me happy.
사진 찍는 것이 나를 행복하게 한다는 것을 깨달았어요.

19. Suddenly, I wanted to become a good photographer, so I started to learn more about photography.
갑자기 좋은 사진작가가 되고 싶어서 사진술에 대해 더 배우기 시작했어요.

20. After years of trial and error, I got better, and I began to do some part-time work as a photographer.
몇 년의 시행착오 끝에 나아졌고, 사진작가로 시간제 근무를 시작했어요.

21. Then one day, I made a bold decision.
그러던 어느 날 나는 대담한 결심을 했어요.

22. I quit my job and decided to take pictures for a living.
직장을 그만두고, 생계 수단으로 사진을 찍기로 했어요.

4-2. 본문 문법 분석 및 Q&A

📘 핵심 문장 분석

① 의문사가 있는 간접의문문

Today, I'm going to tell you how I found my dream and realized it.

★ 직접: How did I find my dream? → 간접: how + I + found (어순 변화)

② 의문사 없는 간접의문문 (if/whether)

I didn't know whether I really enjoyed it.

I wasn't sure if I could succeed, but I decided to try.

③ 동명사구 (~하는 것)

  • I also liked taking pictures. (목적어)
  • Being an engineer looked OK. (주어)
  • In my case, it was taking pictures of stars. (보어)
  • Pursuing a dream is not easy. (주어)

④ to부정사 (형용사적 용법: 명사 수식)

  • There I got a chance to see the Northern Lights.
  • This gave me a chance to think about my life.
  • Here's some advice to help you.

⑤ 5형식 지각동사 (feel + 목적어 + 동사원형/V-ing)

I could feel my heart beating fast.

★ feel + 목적어 + 동사원형(beat) → 동작 반복/진행 / + V-ing(beating) → 동작 일회성/진행 강조

📘 본문 Q&A

Q1. 본문 글의 주인공은 누구이며 직업은 무엇인가요?

▶ The speaker is David Parker, and he is a photographer.

Q2. 밑줄 친 'these things'가 가리키는 것은? (영어로)

▶ ① He loved stars. ② He liked taking pictures.

★ these things = 앞 문장에서 언급된 두 가지 (별 사랑 + 사진 찍기)

Q3. How did David Parker feel when he saw the Northern Lights?

▶ He felt his heart beating fast.

★ feel + 목적어 + 목적격보어(beating) = 5형식 지각동사 구문

Q4. What did David Parker do after he came back from Iceland?

▶ He entered a photo contest with the pictures he took in Iceland.

★ the pictures (that/which) he took = 목적격 관계대명사 생략

✅ 영작 빈칸 채우기

  1. [several / have / show / I / to / during / skills / important / class / the / engineering / students]
    → I have several important skills to show students during the engineering class.
  2. [way / found / he / his / to / success / a / new / to / pursue / quit / job / and]
    → He found a new way to succeed and quit his job to pursue success.
  3. [college / to / her / keep / whether / she / considering / is / job / or / major / change]
    → She is considering whether to keep her job or change her college major.
  4. [camera / surprisingly / belonging / fifty / to / grandfather / suddenly / for / my / started / working / years]
    → Surprisingly, the camera belonging to my grandfather suddenly started working for fifty years.
