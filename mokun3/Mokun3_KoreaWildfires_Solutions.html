Reading Passage · 제시문
(1) In 2025, South Korea experienced devastating wildfires across several regions. The fires first broke out in dry, mountainous areas and spread rapidly because relentless winds continued for three consecutive days. Before firefighters arrived at the scene, the flames had already spread to nearby villages and destroyed 120 houses. According to official reports, a total of 3,150 residents were evacuated to temporary shelters while emergency workers struggled to contain the fires.

(2) Experts explain that climate change continually increases the likelihood of such uncontrollable wildfires and makes them far more intense. Although firefighters worked around the clock, it was extremely difficult to fully suppress the fires. When the flames threatened small towns, local governments proactively closed 15 schools and major roads for safety. In addition, the thick smoke caused serious air pollution, which was highly harmful to people’s health.

(3) After the fires were finally brought under control, the government gathered to discuss more effective ways to prevent future disasters. For instance, experts suggested using AI-powered drones for real-time monitoring and advanced warning systems so that fires could be detected at an early stage. Unless society prepares more thoroughly for natural disasters, similar wildfires may occur again. The 2025 wildfires demonstrated that protecting forests and human lives requires collective responsibility.
