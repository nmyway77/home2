📘 Lesson 3 · 학습 안내

의사소통궁금증 표현 — I'm curious about ~ 「~이 궁금하다」 · 경고·주의 — Make sure (that) ~ 「반드시 ~해라」 / Make sure you don't ~ 「~하지 않도록 해라」.

문법 A과거완료 (had+p.p.) — 과거의 어느 때보다 더 이전에 일어난 일(경험·완료·결과·계속).

문법 B접속사 — although(비록 ~지만) · since(~하기 때문에) · after(~한 후에) 등.

📌 빈칸·선택지·어형 변형 문제는 그대로 두고, 정답과 해설은 어두운 「펼치기」 패널 안에만 담았다. 먼저 스스로 풀어 본 뒤 펼쳐서 확인할 것.

Section A · Listen & Talk 1의사소통 · 궁금증 표현
A-1빈칸·괄호Get Ready 2
빈칸을 채우고, 밑줄 친 that의 쓰임을 고르시오.
B:There a big flood in Europe. Did you hear about it?
G:No, I didn't. But floods aren't that② common in winter, ③ I'm curious about④ ⑤how that happened.
B:Me too. Let's do some online research.
②번 that 은? — 지시어 / 관계대명사 / 접속사
A-1 정답 & 해설 펼치기
ANSWER
There was a big flood · aren't ~, are they? · ②번 that = 지시(부)사
There be단수 주어 a big flood 과거 → was.
부가의문문주절이 부정문(aren't)이므로 긍정 are they? 주어 floods → they.
that형용사 common을 수식하는 지시부사 「그렇게」. (⑤ how that happened는 간접의문문)
A-2빈칸·어형B. Listen and Write
G:There seem many natural disasters in Korea these days.
B:I agree. There was an earthquake in the south last week. Also a storm is coming this week.
G:I'm curious about type of natural disaster causes the most damage in Korea.
B:Actually I read a report yesterday about the damage from each type of natural disaster. Number one is storms.
G:I see. I guess earthquakes are second.
B:No, second is heavy rain, and third is heavy snow.
G:What about earthquakes?
B:[Base]⑥ the report, earthquakes are [fourth / forth]. But the damage from earthquakes [increase]⑦ recently because they [happen]⑨ more often in Korea.
G:I see. It [seem] we have to [prepare] a variety of natural disasters in Korea.
A-2 정답 & 해설 펼치기
ANSWER
There seem to be · which type · Based on · fourth · has been increasing · they have been happening · It seems like · be prepared for
구문There seem to be ~ 「~이 있는 것 같다」, 간접의문문 which type, Based on 「~에 따르면」, 서수 fourth.
시제최근 증가·반복 → 현재완료진행 has been increasing / have been happening.
기타It seems like ~ 「~인 것 같다」, be prepared for 「~에 대비하다」.
A-3빈칸·괄호C. Listen and Speak
B:Hey, did you hear about the big fires in California?
G:No, I didn't. How serious are they?
B:①They've destroyed a large number of② houses and other buildings.
G:Are the fires still going ?
B:Yes, actually the wind has made③ the fires worse. I hope all the people [live] there④ are okay.
G:⑤So I. I'm curious how many people [had to / have to] leave their homes.
B:Actually more than 20,000 people had to leave their homes, and about 400 people are [missing / missed] in that area.
G:That's terrible. I hope they're somewhere safe.
A-3 정답 & 해설 펼치기
ANSWER
still going on · all the people living there · So do I · curious about · had to leave · are missing
표현go on 「계속되다」, 현재분사구 living there가 people 수식, 동의 So do I 「나도 그래」.
기타curious about, 과거 의무 had to leave, 「실종 상태」 are missing.
A-4빈칸D. Talk Together
e.g.
A:I heard that there been many wildfires in Korea.
B:Yes. There was a big one in Yangyang.
A:I'm curious about it happened.
B:It happened in 2005.
A-4 정답 & 해설 펼치기
ANSWER
there have been many wildfires · curious about when it happened
구문현재완료 there have been, 간접의문문 when it happened 「언제 일어났는지」.
Section B · Listen & Talk 2의사소통 · 경고·주의주기
B-1빈칸Get Ready 2
B:Mom, ① do we need to put in the natural disaster survival kit?
W:Well, we need water, some food, and a radio.
B:Anything else, Mom?
W:Oh, that② you include batteries the radio.
B-1 정답 & 해설 펼치기
ANSWER
① what else · make sure that ~ · batteries for the radio
표현what else 「또 다른 무엇」, Make sure that ~ 「반드시 ~해라」, batteries for 「~용 배터리」.
B-2빈칸·괄호B. Listen and Order (CPR)
W:Performing① CPR properly can save someone's life. Here are② the steps for proper CPR. First, check [if / that]③ the person needs help. Tap the person and shout, "Are you okay?" If④ there's no reaction, call 119 for help. Second, listen, look, and feel breathing. If the person not breathing, begin CPR. Make sure⑤ you place your hands in the of⑥ the person's chest. Use your body w to press⑦ harder the chest. After 30 presses, give⑧ the person two bre. Keep doing⑨ CPR [until / by]⑩ help arrives.
B-2 정답 & 해설 펼치기
ANSWER
check that · feel for breathing · person is not · in the middle of · body weight · harder on · two breaths · until help arrives
접속사목적어절 접속사 that(생략 가능), until 「~할 때까지」.
표현feel for breathing, in the middle of 「~의 중앙에」, body weight 「체중」, press harder on, two breaths 「두 번의 숨」.
B-3빈칸C. Listen and Speak (fire drill)
B:Susie, this is a fire drill! Let's go!
G:Okay! The guidelines say that① we have to② line quickly.
B:We're going to the meeting place, right?
G:Right. Remember your body while④ you're walking.
B:I got it. Hey, why don't we⑤ take the elevators? They're faster than the stairs.
G:No. Make sure you take the elevators in a real fire. It's very dangerous.
B:Okay. Wait! I need to get my backpack!
G:Forget your backpack. Leave your belongings and just go.
B:You're right. Okay, let's go!
B-3 정답 & 해설 펼치기
ANSWER
get in line · Remember to lower · Make sure you don't take · Forget about · belongings behind
표현get in line 「줄을 서다」, Remember to + 동사원형 「~할 것을 기억하다」(미래), Make sure you don't ~ 「~하지 않도록 해라」.
동사구Forget about ~, leave ~ behind 「~을 두고 가다」.

B-4 정답 & 해설 펼치기
ANSWER · Talk Together
cover your mouth with a wet cloth · you exit the building · In case of yellow dust · keep the windows closed
ANSWER · Presentation
greatly affected · 80% of New Orleans was underwater · Up to 2,000 people
어휘affect 「~에 영향을 미치다」(동사), keep + 목적어 + closed 「닫힌 채로 유지하다」.
수일치80% of New Orleans(단수 취급) → was, Up to 「~에 달하는」.
Section C · Grammar과거완료 · 접속사
C-1어형POINT A · 과거완료
괄호 안의 단어를 활용하여 문장을 완성하시오.
1. I never pho until I visited Vietnam. (eat)
2. Jin told me how hard he for the match. (prepare)
3. The play already when we arrived. (start)
4. David his glasses earlier, so he couldn't read anything. (lose)
C-1 정답 & 해설 펼치기
ANSWER
1. had, eaten · 2. had prepared · 3. had, started · 4. had lost
과거완료과거의 한 시점(visited, told, arrived, couldn't read)보다 더 이전의 일 → 「had + 과거분사」.
C-2접속사POINT B · 접속사
주어진 접속사 중 알맞은 것을 골라 문장을 완성하시오. [after · although · since]
1. Mina knew the right answer, she didn't let us know.
2. it was snowing heavily, I stayed home all day.
3. Our teacher will edit our video we finish recording it.
C-2 정답 & 해설 펼치기
ANSWER
1. Although · 2. Since · 3. after
접속사Although 「비록 ~지만」(양보), Since 「~하기 때문에」(이유), after 「~한 후에」(시간).
C-3어형·괄호Real-Life Language A
일기 주인공의 하루를 상상하며 괄호 안의 단어를 활용해 완성하시오.
Today everything went wrong. First, I got up late. When I [arrived at / arrived] school, class 1. already (begin). I quietly sat down at my desk. Soon I realized that I 2. (leave) my report at home. Suddenly my teacher asked me to read my report. I 3. never (be) in such a situation, so my face got really red. I want to forget everything about today!
C-3 정답 & 해설 펼치기
ANSWER
arrived at school · 1. had, begun · 2. had left · 3. had, been
동사arrive at 「~에 도착하다」.
과거완료도착·깨달음(과거)보다 더 이전의 일 → had begun / had left / had been.
C-4영작Real-Life Language B
뒤에 이어질 내용을 자유롭게 생각하여 완성하시오. [walk my dog · play soccer · study hard · go to an amusement park · get a discount]
1. After I finish the exam, I .
2. Although it's very hot outside, I .
3. Since I am a student, I .
C-4 예시 답안 펼치기
SAMPLE
1. will walk my dog · 2. will play soccer · 3. will study hard
예시e.g. After I finish the exam, I will go to an amusement park. / Since I am a student, I will get a discount.
Section D · Think and Write재난 영화 비평문 · 어법
D-1어형·괄호San Andreas
[ ] 안의 단어를 어법에 맞게 고치고, 빈칸을 채우시오.
I would like to tell you about the movie San Andreas. This movie [set] Los Angeles and San Francisco in 2014. The main character, a search-and-rescue pilot, must search his [missing / missed] family [during / for] an earthquake. The special effects [use] in the disaster scenes [are / is] very good. The movie is a little sad at times, but the story is very interesting. I give San Andreas four stars. Go and watch it!
D-1 정답 & 해설 펼치기
ANSWER
set→is set in — be set in 「~을 배경으로 하다」.
search __→for — search for 「~을 찾다」.
missed→missing — 「실종된」 능동/진행 의미의 현재분사.
for→during — 특정 기간(an earthquake) 앞 during.
use→used — effects가 「사용된」 것이므로 과거분사.
is→are — 주어 The special effects(복수).
D-2어형·괄호Only the Brave
I would like to tell you about the movie Only the Brave. This movie [set] Yarnell, Arizona in 2013. The Granite Mountain Hotshots, a group of elite firefighters, [risk / risks] everything to protect a town from a historic wildfire. As the movie [base] a true story, it was even [sad] and [touching]. It reminds us the many contributions firefighters make each day. I give Only the Brave five stars. Go and watch it!
D-2 정답 & 해설 펼치기
ANSWER
set→is set in — be set in 「~을 배경으로 하다」.
risks→risk — 주어 The Granite Mountain Hotshots(복수).
base→is based on — be based on 「~에 바탕을 두다」.
sad / touching→sadder and more touching — even 뒤 비교급.
reminds us __→of — remind A of B 「A에게 B를 상기시키다」.
Section E · All the TextAfter You Read · Do It Yourself
E-1번호 쓰기After You Read A
다음 내용을 듣고 누가 한 말인지 알맞은 번호를 쓰시오. (me / Mom / Dad)
1. I pulled my children out of their beds and ran to the kitchen.
2. I woke up suddenly because my bed was shaking.
3. I pulled over and listened to the radio to find out what was going on.
me / Mom / Dad
E-1 정답 & 해설 펼치기
ANSWER
me - 2 · Mom - 1 · Dad - 3
단서침대가 흔들려 잠에서 깬 것은 나(2), 아이들을 끌어낸 것은 엄마(1), 차를 세우고 라디오를 들은 것은 아빠(3).
E-2질문 고르기After You Read B
본문과 일치하도록 빈칸에 알맞은 질문을 골라 쓰시오. (R: Reporter, W: Writer)
R
WI began to panic because the whole room was shaking violently.
RHow scary!
WWe all crawled under the table after my mom got us out of bed.
R
WLots of things were falling to the floor. I heard many things in the apartment break.
R
WI realized that I should be prepared for the next earthquake. It can occur at any time!
a. What did you do next?   b. What was happening at that moment?   c. How did you feel when the earthquake occurred?   d. What did you realize after that night?
E-2 정답 & 해설 펼치기
ANSWER
c · a · b · d
흐름기분(c) → 그 다음 행동(a) → 그때 상황(b) → 이후 깨달음(d) 순으로 인터뷰가 진행된다.

We took the 3. and went to a(n) 4. .
E-3 정답 & 해설 펼치기
ANSWER

E-4고르기Do It Yourself A
대화를 듣고, 대화가 끝난 후에 두 사람이 할 일을 고르시오.
G:Did you hear that earthquakes are occurring more often in Korea than before?
B:Oh, really? I've never felt an earthquake in Korea.
G:They usually occur in the southern part of Korea, but now they are occurring in other places as well.
B:I didn't know that. I'm curious about why earthquakes have occurred so often in Korea recently.
G:Why don't we do some research to find out?
B:Sounds good, but where do we look first?
G:How about asking our science teacher first? I think she can help us.
B:Okay. Let's go and find her.
a. 지진 체험관 방문하기   b. 인터넷 검색하기   c. 과학 선생님 찾아가기
E-4 정답 & 해설 펼치기
ANSWER
c
단서「How about asking our science teacher first?」 → 과학 선생님을 찾아가기로 함.
E-5심경 고르기Do It Yourself B

But then I heard the mirror on my desk fall to the floor and break into pieces. I knew it wasn't my brother then, but I still didn't know what exactly was happening.

a. angry   b. excited   c. sad   d. confused
E-5 정답 & 해설 펼치기
ANSWER
d (confused, 혼란스러운)
단서동생 장난인 줄 알았다가 거울이 깨지는 소리에 무슨 일인지 정확히 모르는 상태 → 혼란스러운(d).
E-6접속사Do It Yourself C
밑줄 친 부분에 들어갈 접속사를 골라 게시물을 완성하시오. [although · after · since]
Last weekend, my sister and I had planned to go on a picnic, but we changed our plans the yellow dust was really bad. we couldn't go outside, we had a good time. we finished dinner, we watched a movie together.
E-6 정답 & 해설 펼치기
ANSWER
since · Although · After
접속사황사가 심해서(이유) since, 밖에 못 나갔지만(양보) Although, 저녁 식사 후(시간) After.
