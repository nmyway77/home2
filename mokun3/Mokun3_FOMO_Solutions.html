Reading Passage · 제시문
(1) People in modern society frequently pursue trends despite the possibility that their popularity may fade. One explanation for this phenomenon is the fear of missing out, commonly known as FOMO, which motivates individuals to follow popular trends to avoid social exclusion. It was originally used as a marketing term to encourage consumers to purchase products through phrases like “limited quantity” and “almost sold out” by appealing to people’s fear of being left out.

(2) People experiencing FOMO often feel uneasy as they believe they are excluded. In severe cases, FOMO can become psychologically destructive, as it causes social media addiction and impulsive purchases driven by the fear of missing trends. A recent survey reported that a number of teenagers purchase products because they became viral online. Nevertheless, people seldom recognize how strongly social media algorithms influence their decisions and consumption patterns.

(3) In some situations, trends can be helpful in that people feel connected to larger communities and may obtain advantages or profits by recognizing them early before they attract widespread public attention. However, individuals should develop the ability to evaluate trends critically instead of accepting them unconditionally. In addition, accepting their own lives and circumstances without excessive comparison may help people alleviate their negative feelings and maintain psychological stability.

ANSWER · 정답
정답 ②
