Unit 28 · 가정법 현재와 과거

A. 가정법 현재

‘만일 ~라면 …할 것이다’ — 현재·미래의 단순한 가정. 「If+주어+동사의 현재형 ~, 주어+will/shall/can/may+동사원형 …」
If you are tired, you can go home now.
If it is very cold tomorrow, I’ll not go out.

B. 가정법 현재 명사절

제안(suggest, propose)·주장(insist, urge)·명령(order)·추천(recommend)·요구(demand, ask, inquire, request) 동사의 that절에는 「(should)+동사원형」이 오며 should는 생략 가능.
He asked that I (should) tell him the truth.
She demanded that he (should) clean the room.
He proposed that we (should) go fishing tomorrow.

C. 가정법 과거

1. ‘만일 ~라면 …할 텐데’ — 현재 사실과 반대. 「If+주어+과거형 ~, 주어+would/should/could/might+동사원형 …」 (if절 be동사는 인칭 무관 were)
As we don’t have a car, we can’t go on a vacation. → If we had a car, we would go on a vacation.
As I am not rich, I can’t buy the house. → If I were rich, I would buy the house.
2. if절 동사가 were일 때 if를 생략하고 주어와 동사의 위치를 도치할 수 있다.
If she were honest, she would tell me the truth. → Were she honest, she would tell me the truth.

Unit 29 · 가정법 과거완료·미래·혼합가정법

A. 가정법 과거완료

1. ‘만일 ~했다면 …했을 것이다’ — 과거 사실과 반대. 「If+주어+had+과거분사 ~, 주어+would/should/could/might+have+과거분사 …」
As I didn’t work hard, I couldn’t become rich. → If I had worked hard, I would have become rich.
If you had arrived ten minutes later, you couldn’t have caught the last train.
2. if를 생략하고 주어와 had를 도치할 수 있다.
If we had had a car, we would have gone on a vacation. = Had we had a car, we would have gone on a vacation.

B. 가정법 미래

1. 「If+주어+should+동사원형 ~, 주어+would/will/should/shall/could/can/might/may+동사원형 …」 현재·미래에 대한 강한 의심. (if 생략 시 should 도치)
If it should be true, I would be glad. = Should it be true, I would be glad.
2. 「If+주어+were to+동사원형 ~, …」 미래에 실현 불가능한 일을 가정. (조건 ‘~한다면’ / 양보 ‘~하더라도’)
If the sun were to rise in the west, I would believe what you said.

C. 혼합가정법

과거 사실이 현재까지 영향을 미치는 경우. 「If+주어+had+과거분사 ~(과거), 주어+would/should/could/might+동사원형 …(현재)」
If you had not helped me yesterday, I would not be alive now.
(어제 네가 나를 도와주지 않았더라면, 나는 지금 살아있지 않을 것이다.)

Unit 30 · 가정법 특수구문가정법 특수구문
Unit 30 · 가정법 특수구문

A. I wish + 가정법 과거 / 과거완료

1. I wish+가정법 과거: ‘~하면 좋을 텐데’ 현재의 실현 불가능한 소망. → 「I’m sorry / It’s a pity+직설법 현재」
I wish I were young again. → I am sorry I am not young again.
2. I wish+가정법 과거완료: ‘~했더라면 좋았을 텐데’ 과거의 이루지 못한 소망. → 「I’m sorry / It’s a pity+직설법 과거」
I wish I had had enough time to read the novel. → I am sorry I didn’t have enough time to read the novel.

B. as if (as though) + 가정법 과거 / 과거완료

1. 「주어+현재형+as if+주어+과거형」 ‘마치 ~인 것처럼 …하다’ (현재 시점 · 현재 사실 반대)
She feels as if she were in a dream. → In fact, she isn’t in a dream.
2. 「주어+현재형+as if+주어+had+p.p.」 ‘마치 ~이었던 것처럼’ (현재 시점 · 과거 사실 반대)
She feels as if she had been in a dream. → In fact, she wasn’t in a dream.
3. 「주어+과거형+as if+주어+과거형」 (과거 시점 · 과거 사실 반대) / 4. 「주어+과거형+as if+주어+had+p.p.」 (과거 시점 · 그 이전 사실 반대)

C. 조건절의 대용어구

1. ‘~이 없다면 …할 텐데’ (가정법 과거): If it were not for ~ = Were it not for ~ = Without ~ = But for ~
If it were not for water, no fish could live. = Were it not for ~ = Without ~ = But for water, no fish could live.
2. ‘~이 없었다면 …했을 텐데’ (가정법 과거완료): If it had not been for ~ = Had it not been for ~ = Without ~ = But for ~
If it had not been for your help, I would have failed. = Had it not been for ~ = Without ~ = But for your help, I would have failed.
3. if절의 대용: To hear him talk, you would take him for an American.(=If you heard) / Turning to the right, you will find the store.(=If you turn) / I feel better; otherwise I would have to see the doctor.(=If I didn’t feel better)

D. It is (about / high) time + (that) + 가정법 과거

‘~해야 할 때다’ 「It is (about/high) time (that)+주어+과거형」 = 「~ should+동사원형」 = 「~ for+사람+to부정사」
It is time you went to school. = It is time you should go to school. = It is time for you to go to school.

I’m sorry I didn’t see you at the party. = I wish I ______ you at the party.
정답 펼치기
Answer
had seen

Reference · 독립부정사 (Independent Infinitive)
문장 전체를 수식하는 독립부정사 관용 표현 정리. (제16장 부록)
To tell the truth, I don’t like him that much. 〈사실을 말하자면〉
To make a long story short / In a word / In short(brief), I found the book interesting. 〈간단히 말하자면〉
He can play the flute, not to mention / to say nothing of / not to speak of / let alone the piano. 〈…은 말할 나위도 없이〉
To begin with / First of all / Above all / To start with / Before getting started, I’d like to thank you for coming. 〈우선, 첫째로〉
To make matters worse / What is worse / What makes matters worse, I had acrophobia. 〈설상가상으로〉 (acrophobia 고소공포증)
He is, so to speak / that is to say, kindness itself. 〈말하자면〉
Strange to say, his advice encouraged me greatly. 〈말하기 이상하지만〉
